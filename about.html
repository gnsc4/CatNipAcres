About Catnip Acres

Our history, mission, and commitment to affordable veterinary care

Our History

Catnip Acres began in 2007 as a cat rescue that conducted high-volume/low-cost cat spay and neuter clinics. We've since evolved into a high-quality community care veterinary clinic.

Catnip Acres is proud to provide affordable veterinary care for both cats and dogs, while continuing to honor our original mission for the betterment of the feline population.

Our clinic has grown from humble beginnings to become a trusted veterinary care provider in the Waynesburg area. Through the years, we've maintained our commitment to accessible care while expanding our services to meet the needs of our community.

Our Mission

Our mission is to ameliorate the suffering associated with cat overpopulation in Southwestern PA, specifically Greene County. Catnip Acres reaches an under-served subset of animals, providing high-volume/low-cost sterilization programs for cats.

Through sterilization and vaccination, Catnip Acres increases animal quality of life and fights overpopulation, thereby decreasing animal abuse, neglect, disease, and shelter over-crowding.

We believe that every pet deserves quality veterinary care, regardless of their owner's financial situation. By offering affordable services, we help ensure that more animals receive the care they need.

Our Values

Compassion

We treat every animal with the love and care they deserve, understanding that they are beloved family members.

Affordability

We believe financial constraints should never prevent a pet from receiving necessary medical care.

Quality

We provide high-quality care using modern techniques and equipment, never compromising on the standard of care.

Community

We are committed to serving our community and addressing the specific needs of pets and pet owners in our area.

Important Note

THE CLINIC IS NOT A RESCUE. Please do not contact the clinic about taking cats to put up for adoption, we are not involved in that process. Visit the website catnipacresrescue.org for more information about cat adoptions.
