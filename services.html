Wellness Exams

Regular check-ups and preventative care to keep your pet healthy. We offer comprehensive physical examinations to detect any potential health issues early.

During a wellness exam, our veterinarian will:

  • Perform a thorough nose-to-tail examination
  • Check vital signs (temperature, heart rate, respiratory rate)
  • Assess body condition and weight
  • Examine eyes, ears, teeth, and coat condition
  • Discuss nutrition, behavior, and exercise
  • Recommend appropriate vaccinations and preventative care

Regular wellness exams are essential for catching health issues before they become serious, especially since pets can't tell us when they're not feeling well. We recommend annual exams for adult pets and semi-annual exams for senior pets (7+ years).

Vaccinations

Protection against common and serious diseases. We provide core and lifestyle vaccinations for both cats and dogs tailored to their specific needs.

Vaccinations are an essential part of preventative healthcare for your pet. They stimulate the immune system to create protection against infectious diseases, many of which can be life-threatening.

For dogs, we recommend:

  • Rabies - Required by law and protects against the fatal rabies virus
  • DHPP - Protects against distemper, hepatitis, parainfluenza, and parvovirus
  • Bordetella - Prevents kennel cough, recommended for dogs that visit groomers, boarding facilities, or dog parks
  • Leptospirosis - Protects against a bacterial infection that can cause kidney and liver damage
  • Lyme - Recommended for dogs in areas with high tick populations

For cats, we recommend:

  • Rabies - Required by law and protects against the fatal rabies virus
  • FVRCP - Protects against feline viral rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, and panleukopenia
  • FeLV - Recommended for outdoor cats or those living with FeLV-positive cats

Spay & Neuter

Low-cost sterilization services to help control the pet population and improve your pet's health and behavior. We specialize in high-volume/low-cost cat spay and neuter procedures.

About Our Spay & Neuter Services

Our spay and neuter clinics typically run on Fridays and Saturdays by appointment only. We are committed to providing affordable, high-quality spay/neuter services to help reduce pet overpopulation and improve the health of pets in our community.

What to Expect

When you schedule a spay or neuter appointment for your pet:

  • Morning drop-off between 8:00-9:00 AM
  • Same-day pickup in the afternoon (typically between 3:00-4:00 PM)
  • Pre-anesthetic examination
  • General anesthesia with careful monitoring throughout the procedure
  • Pain medication during surgery and for home use
  • Written post-operative care instructions

Pre-Surgery Instructions

To ensure your pet's safety during surgery:

  • No food after midnight the night before surgery (water is OK)
  • Bring cats in secure carriers (one cat per carrier)
  • Dogs should be on leashes
  • Current rabies vaccination required (can be given day of surgery for additional fee)
  • Please inform us of any medical conditions or medications your pet is taking

Recommended Ages

We recommend spaying and neutering at the following ages:

  • Cats: 3-4 months of age (minimum 2 pounds)
  • Dogs: 4-6 months of age (breed-dependent)

Post-Operative Care

After surgery, your pet will need:

  • A quiet, comfortable place to recover
  • Limited activity for 7-10 days
  • No bathing for 10-14 days
  • Daily incision checks
  • Prevention of licking at the incision (E-collar may be required)
  • Administration of any prescribed pain medications

We'll provide detailed written instructions for post-operative care when you pick up your pet.

Benefits of spaying/neutering include:

  • Prevention of unwanted litters
  • Reduced risk of certain cancers
  • Decreased roaming behavior
  • Reduced aggression in males
  • Prevention of heat cycles in females
