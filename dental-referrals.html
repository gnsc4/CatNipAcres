Dental Referrals

Professional dental care for your pet's oral health

At Catnip Acres Veterinary Clinic, we understand the importance of dental health for your pet's overall wellbeing. Poor dental health can lead to pain, infection, and even systemic health issues that affect your pet's quality of life.

We provide basic dental examinations and care recommendations as part of our regular services, but for more complex dental procedures, we work with specialized veterinary dental partners to ensure your pet receives the best possible care.

Our Dental Services

Dental Examinations

Regular dental check-ups are included in our wellness exams. Our veterinarian will examine your pet's teeth and gums to assess their oral health and identify any issues that may need attention.

Basic Dental Services

At Catnip Acres, we offer the following basic dental services:

  • Visual dental examinations
  • Dental health assessments
  • Basic dental cleaning (scaling and polishing)
  • Simple extractions
  • Home dental care recommendations

When Referrals Are Needed

We may recommend a referral to a veterinary dental specialist for:

  • Complex extractions
  • Root canal therapy
  • Treatment of severe periodontal disease
  • Oral surgeries
  • Treating pets with special health considerations

Our Referral Process

Initial Assessment

Our veterinarian performs a comprehensive oral examination to determine if your pet requires specialized dental care beyond what we provide at our clinic.

Referral Preparation

We prepare a detailed referral with your pet's medical history, examination findings, and any relevant diagnostic results to share with the dental specialist.

Specialist Selection

We help you select an appropriate veterinary dental specialist based on your pet's needs, your location, and your budget considerations.

Follow-Up Care

After your pet receives specialized dental treatment, we provide necessary follow-up care and monitor their recovery and ongoing dental health.

Signs Your Pet May Need Dental Care

Bad breath
Red or swollen gums
Yellow or brown buildup on teeth
Difficulty eating or dropping food
Excessive drooling
Pawing at the mouth
Bleeding from the mouth
Loss of appetite or weight

Important: If you notice any of these signs, please schedule a dental examination for your pet as soon as possible. Early detection and treatment of dental problems can prevent more serious health issues.

Home Dental Care Tips

Brushing

Regular brushing with pet-specific toothpaste is the most effective way to maintain your pet's dental health at home. Aim for daily brushing if possible.

Dental Treats and Toys

Dental chews, treats, and toys designed to reduce plaque and tartar can supplement your pet's dental care routine.

Dental Diet

Specialized dental diets can help reduce plaque and tartar buildup through the mechanical action of kibble or added ingredients.

Water Additives

Dental water additives can help reduce bacteria in your pet's mouth and freshen their breath.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should my pet have a dental cleaning?

Most pets benefit from a professional dental cleaning once a year, though some may need more frequent cleanings depending on their individual oral health needs and breed predispositions.

Does my pet need to be anesthetized for dental procedures?

Yes, proper dental cleanings and treatments require anesthesia to ensure your pet remains still and comfortable. This allows for thorough cleaning below the gumline and complete examination of all teeth and oral tissues.

What should I expect during the referral process?

We'll provide you with a referral to one of our trusted dental partners, share your pet's records, and help coordinate the appointment. After the specialist treatment, we'll schedule follow-up care at our clinic.

Is dental care covered by pet insurance?

Many pet insurance policies cover dental procedures, especially when related to accidents or illness. Routine dental cleanings may be covered under wellness plans. We recommend checking with your specific insurance provider.
